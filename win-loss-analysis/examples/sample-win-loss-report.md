# Win/Loss Analysis — Acme — April 14, 2026

**Owner:** GTM Lead  ·  **Next review:** May 14, 2026  ·  **Confidence:** High on firmographics & persona, Medium on industry and association coverage

---

## 🎯 TL;DR — What The Data Actually Says

1. **The stated ICP is the anti-ICP.** Acme's documented target (50–500 emp, RevOps/GTM Ops) wins at 8–28%. The actual winning segment (1–10 emp, founder/CEO) wins at 71–74%. This is not a rounding error — it's a two-motion business being run as one.
2. **Speed is the #1 predictor of winning.** Deals that close in <7 days win 90% (n=260). After 60 days, win rate collapses to 25–31%. The stale threshold is 14 days.
3. **Founders buy. Ops evaluates.** Any title containing "founder", "ceo", or "president" wins 71–80%. Any title containing "operations", "manager", "director", or "vp" wins 12–31%. Persona is destiny.
4. **OFFLINE + self-serve is the revenue engine.** 70% WR and 71% WR respectively. Organic search (7% WR) and direct traffic (26% WR) bring in wrong-fit visitors.
5. **Association coverage is the hidden bias.** Won deals have only 66% contact coverage and ~53% company coverage, so the small-founder signal in the firmographic tables is likely understated, not overstated.

---

## ✅ Strategic Recommendations (Top 3 Actions)

1. **Split the ICP into two motions.** Motion A = 1–50 emp, founder buyer, self-serve, <14-day cycle. Motion B = 50–500 emp, ops buyer, sales-assist (currently unproven — treat as a growth bet, not the core).
2. **Add title-based routing at lead creation.** Titles with "founder/ceo/president/owner" → fast-track. Titles with "operations/ops/manager/director/vp/sales" → nurture, not sales. This alone would raise blended WR materially.
3. **Set a 14-day stale deal alert and audit the open-deal backlog.** Win rate falls below 50% after 15 days. Most of the backlog is already dead — surfacing that is the fastest path to a clean pipeline and real forecasting.

## 🔥 Headline Finding — Stated ICP vs Winning Motion

> Acme's documented ICP targets 50–500 employee companies with RevOps/GTM Ops buyers. In the CRM data, that segment wins **8–28%** of the time. The segment that actually wins — 1–10 employee companies with founder/CEO buyers — wins **71–74%** and represents **42% of all won deals with firmographic data**.
>
> **Implication:** Either (a) update the ICP to match the founder-led self-serve motion that is already working, or (b) treat mid-market as a distinct Motion B with its own playbook, sales cycle, and proof assets. Running one motion against two buyers is why 43.5% of losses have no recorded reason — the sales team doesn't know why mid-market deals die because the product isn't built for them.

## ⚠️ Association Coverage — Why Some Deals Don't Appear In Firmographic Tables

A reasonable question when reading this report: *the funnel says 376 won deals, but the firmographic tables only reference ~200 companies. What's going on?*

**The answer is orphan deals.** HubSpot does not require a deal to be linked to a company or contact record. In Acme's CRM:

| Dataset | Records | Covered | Coverage |
|---|---|---|---|
| Won deals → associated companies | 376 | ~200 | **~53%** |
| Won deals → associated contacts | 376 | 248 | **66%** |
| Lost deals → associated companies | 207 | 198 | **96%** |
| Lost deals → associated contacts | 207 | 188 | **91%** |

**Why the gap concentrates on won deals:** The self-serve / PLG signup flow creates deals directly from signup events without always attaching a company or contact record. These are mostly small founder-led purchases that close instantly — exactly the segment with the highest win rate. Lost deals, by contrast, are mostly sales-assisted and therefore manually enriched with company/contact records before anyone marks them lost.

**What this means for the analysis:**

- The firmographic and persona tables are **biased toward the mid-market / sales-assisted motion**, because that's where association coverage is complete.
- The **1–10 emp founder signal is almost certainly UNDERSTATED**. The orphan deals we couldn't attribute are disproportionately small founder-led wins — which would push the "1–10 emp" bucket even higher and the "founder/CEO" persona even stronger.
- **Directional read:** every firmographic/persona signal in this report should be read as a floor, not a ceiling, for the small/founder segment.

**What to fix:** Require company + primary contact association at deal creation in HubSpot workflows. A simple required-association automation would close this gap going forward.
